# Administrator guide

[繁體中文](../admin-guide.md) · [Documentation home](../README.md) · [User workflows](user-guide.md)

This guide focuses on proposal review, status updates, announcements, and routine administration. Backend policy remains authoritative; visible controls do not bypass content visibility.

## Before you begin

- Sign in with an allowed-domain account included in the administrator list.
- Sign in again after a role change so the new role reaches the current token.
- Review category visibility, author display, support thresholds, and response deadlines.
- Keep private proposals and user data out of public content and screenshots.

## Review proposals

1. Open a proposal awaiting review.
2. Check its category, suitability for publication, personal information, and media.
3. Approve it, or provide a specific and actionable rejection reason.
4. After approval, verify visibility, author display, and support behavior.

Review decides whether content enters the next platform stage. It does not replace formal school policy, appeals, or emergency reporting channels.

## Update processing status

Move accepted work to processing. When closing it, choose completed or infeasible and write an outcome users can understand. Do not use a status in place of necessary private communication or publish unconfirmed promises.

| Status | Management focus |
| --- | --- |
| Pending review | Check publication risk and category first |
| Processing | Keep progress and response targets traceable |
| Completed | Describe completed work and verifiable results |
| Infeasible | Explain constraints, reasons, and possible alternatives |

## Publish announcements

Published announcements cannot be edited and may be broadcast through in-app notifications and Web Push. Recheck the title, Markdown, images, links, dates, and audience before publishing. If an announcement is wrong, delete and republish it using the platform's current capabilities.

## Dashboard and routine work

The dashboard summarizes activity, category outcomes, and background integration health; it is not an audit log. Check pending reviews, approaching response targets, unusual activity, notifications, images, and external synchronization on a regular cadence.

Use the [operations runbook](operations.md) for incidents and vendor status, and [troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md) for common behavior or display problems.

## Security and handoff

- Update the administrator list when responsibilities change and require a fresh sign-in.
- Never share administrator accounts or paste production credentials into proposals or comments.
- Stop and follow the [private security process](security.md#reporting-a-vulnerability) after suspected unauthorized access, disclosure, or sign-in.
- Hand off pending reviews, active proposals, and known incidents—not only account access.
